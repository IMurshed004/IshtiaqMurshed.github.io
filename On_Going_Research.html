Molecular Dynamics Simulations of Carbon Nanotube-Reinforced Polymer Matrices for High-Performance Aerospace Materials

July 2025 – Present
Ishtiaq Murshed Mehedi Hasan

Carbon nanotube (CNT)–reinforced polymer composites offer exceptional mechanical and thermal properties that make them prime candidates for next-generation aerospace materials. In this work, all-atom molecular dynamics simulations were carried out in LAMMPS to probe the interfacial load transfer, dispersion effects, and failure mechanisms of CNTs embedded in epoxy and PEEK matrices under tensile and thermal loading. The simulations reveal that well-dispersed, functionalized CNTs can increase the composite’s tensile strength and stiffness while also improving thermal conductivity, providing critical design guidelines for high-performance aerospace applications.

Solar PV Panels’ Waste Material Recycling: Bangladesh Perspective

April 2025 – Present
Ishtiaq Murshed, Faridul Islam, Sadman Anjum Joarder, Mehedi Hasan

Bangladesh’s solar photovoltaic (PV) sector has experienced rapid growth, with installed capacity projected to exceed 1,500 MW by mid-2025, leading to a forecasted surge in end-of-life panel waste that will peak between 2042 and 2045. The study systematically reviews current recycling technologies—mechanical, thermal, chemical, and hybrid approaches—assessing their material recovery efficiencies and operational constraints. It contrasts the environmental risks of improper disposal, including heavy‐metal soil and water contamination, GHG emissions, and landfill pressures, with the economic benefits of reclaiming glass, Al, Si, and Cu. Policy analysis reveals a critical gap in PV-specific recycling regulations under Bangladesh’s general e-waste framework, prompting recommendations for dedicated collection systems, licensed recycling facilities, and integration with established industrial processes.

Technological Advancements in Downstream Processing of Critical/Value Minerals Tellurium (Te) from 2nd-Generation CdTe solar cells and optimization of Te’s usage in CdTe solar cells by SCAPS- 1D Simulation study

April 2025 – Present
Bakhtiar Azim, Fahimul Islam, Ishtiaq Murshed

This study reviews recent technological advancements in downstream processing of Te from end-of-life CdTe modules, encompassing hydrometallurgical leaching, solvent extraction, ion-exchange, and pyrometallurgical techniques, and evaluates their recovery yields and environmental footprints. A process flow combining the most effective unit operations is proposed to maximize Te reclamation while minimizing chemical consumption and waste. In parallel, SCAPS-1D simulation is employed to optimize the Te absorber layer’s thickness and doping concentration in CdTe cells, demonstrating that Te usage can be significantly reduced without compromising photovoltaic performance.
